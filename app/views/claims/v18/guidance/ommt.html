Claim for Oliver McGowan Mandatory Training (OMMT) - draft

Adult social care organisations can now claim reimbursement for Oliver McGowan Mandatory Training on Learning Disability and Autism (OMMT) from the Learning and Development Support Scheme (LDSS).

Your organisation must be registered with the Care Quality Commission (CQC) to be eligible.

Funding for OMMT is ring-fenced and is in addition to the LDSS funding available for other courses and qualifications. There is also a separate funding cap per organisation for OMMT - you’ll find details in the LDSS guide for employers.

OMMT is delivered in 2 tiers: tier 1 and tier 2. While part 1 of each tier is free, there is a cost for part 2. Funding is available for part 2 courses through the LDSS.

Reimbursement amounts differ according to how the training is delivered. Funding is available for:

  • OMMT courses delivered internally by a member of your organisation’s staff
  • courses procured externally from an approved OMMT training provider

In both cases you'll need to provide evidence of payment and evidence of completion. Evidence of payment for internally delivered training could include:

  • proof of payment to an awarding organisation for learner registration and certification fees
  • proof of payment to a third-party training subsidiary

All OMMT courses must be delivered by an approved OMMT training provider in order to be eligible for LDSS funding - see a list of approved trainers.

If one learner completes several courses you will need to submit a separate claim for each course. Similarly, if several learners complete the same course, you will need to claim separately for each learner.

Providing evidence of completion

As with other courses reimbursed through LDSS, you'll need to provide a certificate of completion for each learner in order to receive reimbursement.

OMMT training providers do not automatically issue certificates of completion so you may need to request them.

You’ll need to check that the certificates show:

  • a completion date that is within the agreed claims window - you’ll find this in the guide for employers.
  • which tier of training has been completed
  • the trainer’s name and registration number
