Claim for adult social care learning and development

Care providers and local authorities in England can use this service to get reimbursed for part of the costs of learning and development for staff working in adult social care.

Find out how you can use the service to claim for Oliver McGowan Mandatory Training (OMMT).

The amount you get back may be lower than the course or qualification cost.

You can:

  • find an eligible training course or qualification using the scheme guidance
  • claim back the cost

You cannot book training using this service. You'll need to book directly with the training provider.

You can claim the costs of training courses and qualifications for:

  • staff who provide direct care and are not registered with a professional body
  • registered managers, including aspiring managers who are working towards registration

You'll find more information in the guide for employers.

Start now

Before you start

You'll need to have an account to use this reimbursement service.

The service is currently being tested. If you have not had an invitation you will not yet be able to use it.

Your organisation must also have an Adult Social Care Workforce Data Set account – see Skills for Care for details.

To make a claim you'll need to be able to enter:

  • what the activity was
  • the details of who completed the activity
  • evidence relating to the activity

You will need to provide evidence of payment for the activity. For training courses and qualifications, you'll also need to provide evidence of completion.

You will not need to enter all the information in one go. You can start a claim and return to it later.
