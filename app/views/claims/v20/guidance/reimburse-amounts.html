How we calculate reimbursement amounts

Every eligible training course and qualification has a maximum reimbursement amount for each learner. You’ll find these amounts in the list of eligible training courses and qualifications (opens in new tab).

How it works

The maximum reimbursement amount is the most you could get back per learner. It’s usually less than the training actually costs, which means you’ll cover the difference.

For example, if you paid £200 and the maximum reimbursement amount is £160, you'll get back £160 for every learner on a claim.

If you paid less than the maximum reimbursement amount, you’ll get back what you paid.

For example, if you paid £150 and the maximum reimbursement amount is £160, you'll get back £150 per learner.

This does not apply to claims for in-house Oliver McGowan Mandatory Training (OMMT), which has a fixed reimbursement rate.

Annual updates to reimbursement rates

Maximum reimbursement rates are updated on 1 April at the start of each financial year.

This means you may be managing claims for courses that were paid for in different financial years. If this is the case, you may notice that the maximum reimbursement amount differs depending on which year you paid in.
