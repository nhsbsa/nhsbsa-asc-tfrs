Background

The 2021 People at the Heart of Care white paper set out the government’s ambition to provide more training and qualifications to the adult social care workforce. This reimbursement service will support this aim by building on the previous Workforce Development Fund and making it even easier for providers to claim for the costs of training and CPD.

Provider eligibility

To be eligible to use this service, providers must be registered with the Adult Social Care Workforce Data Set (ASC-WDS) and keep their account up to date. An up to date account:

  • has been created or updated within the last financial year
  • has relevant information added for at least 90% of staff

Approved courses

The list of approved training courses and qualifications for staff who provide direct care ensures the highest quality provision of training. It is maintained and regularly reviewed by the Department of Health and Social Care.

Contact the NHS Business Services Authority (NHS BSA) if you think additional courses should be added to the list.

Qualifications

Payment for qualifications will be made in 2 parts. 60% of the cost will be paid on enrolment, with the remaining 40% on completion.

Qualifications must be provided by an accredited awarding body. All qualification levels are eligible.

Evidence

To prevent fraud and ensure the service is used appropriately, NHS BSA requires evidence of activities being claimed for.

Payment evidence

This is required for all claims. It would normally be an invoice that clearly shows the amount being claimed for and the learners who did the activity.

Enrolment evidence

This is required for qualifications. It could be an email or other document showing that the learner has been accepted onto the course.

Completion evidence

This is required for all claims relating to direct care, plus those for professional revalidation that relate to courses or events. It could be an individual certificate or an extract from an electronic register, such as a learning management system (LMS).

Processing and payments

Once claims are submitted, NHS BSA will check them for eligibility and accuracy. If there are any queries about a claim, usually relating to the evidence, you will be able to respond within the service.

NHS BSA will then issue payment, which will be made on a regular basis. This means that there could be a delay before payment reaches you. You will be able to use the service to check the status of claims and find out when payment is expected.

Fraud

NHS BSA has rigorous processes in place to identify fraudulent claims. This could include claims for:

  • learners who do not exist or who are no longer in the provider’s employment
  • activities which did not take place
  • activities which have already been claimed for
  • activities which did not support someone’s professional re-validation

Providers who make fraudulent claims will be required to pay back the amount of the claim and may be prevented from further use of this service.
