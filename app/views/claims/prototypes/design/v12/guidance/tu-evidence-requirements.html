Help with care skills evidence requirements

Adding appropriate evidence to a claim will help to ensure you get reimbursement as quickly as possible. This guidance will help you to do that.

Watch this short video or read the transcript below to understand how to submit evidence appropriately.

Video transcript: Care skills evidence requirements

Evidence requirements

There are 2 types of evidence you’ll need to add when creating a new care skills claim.

  • Evidence of payment
  • Evidence of completion

These are necessary to ensure claims are accurate and to protect against fraud. Without this evidence the NHS Business Services Authority will not be able to process the claim and you will not get back any money.

Each piece of evidence must be:

  • in PDF, JPG, JPEG or PNG format
  • an original document
  • clear and in focus
  • in colour
  • not cropped (you must show the whole document and not just part of it)
  • not altered by computer software
  • not more than 2MB

If you do not have a document in digital form, you should scan or take a photo of it.

Evidence of payment

You need to show that you have paid for the training. The evidence must include:

  • course or qualification name
  • training provider name
  • how much you paid
  • when you made the payment

If you paid in more than one instalment, you will need to provide evidence of the total cost.

You can upload up to 4 separate documents if a single document does not show all necessary information.

Acceptable evidence includes an invoice with associated receipt. An invoice by itself will not be sufficient as it does not show you have actually paid.

Evidence of completion

You also need to show that the learner completed the course or qualification.

Acceptable evidence would be a completion certificate from the training provider. This must include the:
  • date that the training took place or started
  • learner’s name
  • training provider or awarding body
