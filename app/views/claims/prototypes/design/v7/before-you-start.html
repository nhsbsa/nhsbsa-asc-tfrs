Claim for adult social care training and development

Care providers and local authorities in England can use this service to get reimbursed for the costs of training and development for staff working in adult social care. You can:

  • find a relevant training course, qualification or revalidation activity using our eligibility checker
  • claim back the cost

You cannot book training using this service. You'll need to book directly with the training provider.

There are 2 types of funding:

Care skills funding

You can claim the costs of training courses and qualifications for:

  • staff who provide direct care and are not registered with a professional body
  • registered managers, including aspiring managers who are working towards registration

Revalidation funding

You can claim the costs of activities relating to professional revalidation (maintaining a pin) for:

  • nurses registered with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC)
  • allied health professionals registered with the Health & Care Professions Council (HCPC)

Before you start

You'll need to have an account to use this reimbursement service. You can register for one if you need to.

You must also have an up to date Adult Social Care Workforce Data Set account for your organisation.

To make a claim you'll need to be able to enter:

  • what the activity was
  • the details of the people who completed the activity
  • evidence relating to the activity

You will need to provide evidence of payment for the activity, for example an invoice. For training courses and qualifications, you will need to provide evidence of completing the course. For qualifications, you will also need to submit evidence of enrolment.

You will not need to enter all the information in one go. You can start a claim and then return to complete missing information later.
